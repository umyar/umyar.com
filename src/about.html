TLDR
So who are you?
My name is Umiar. And my soft skills are stronger than my hard skills. I'd like to keep developing both my hard and soft skills with equal intensity, but in life I mostly lean on the soft ones. They're exactly what helps me level up the hard ones.
How could you interest me?

FAQ
Umiar, are you open to job offers right now?
Naturally! I'm always open to genuinely interesting offers. If yours stands out, I promise to give it serious thought.
What's important to you?
First and foremost, the chance to grow. Salary comes only after that. Ideally you'd find a balance between incredible growth opportunities and a great salary. A huge plus on your side would be the chance to throw me into an English-speaking environment — that alone would put serious weight on the scales in your favor.
What does growth mean to you right now?
That's hard to answer in one line. We'll most likely have to talk it through if your role is close to the options in the TLDR.
The most exciting thing right now is trying myself in some new position. I feel a strong pull, and enough in me, to finally try the role of team lead. I had the chance before but turned it down because I wasn't mentally ready (an offer before Helios Companies). In the end I came around to it anyway (Helios offered me a lead role). So now is the perfect time. I feel I might be even better at leading than at coding.
For a leadership role I'm fine with a bare-minimum salary. If I'm interesting to your team as a developer, I'm happy to join too, but then there are two conditions. First, I'll want a salary in my comfortable range. Second, after a certain amount of time I should have the chance to grow into a lead with you. Maybe you have a team that needs a developer who doubles as a scrum master — that's me too.
What about the salary range and formalities?
My comfortable salary range is $6500–7000. The work schedule needs to be flexible. As for employment format, I'm equally happy with a sole-proprietor arrangement or a full-time staff contract.
Why did you leave your last job?
When I quit, I wasn't able to properly put into words what I wanted and expected from work. I've got quite a few conflicting thoughts about it now, so the reflection continues. Results in the next episode. Stay tuned.
What was the project at your last job?
A SaaS in high-risk compliance. Within one product there were two projects. One in production, the second almost shipped. I worked on both. More details in my CV (Habr Career, hh, Linkedin).
How do you rate yourself? Middle/Senior?
This is where it gets interesting. I'm sure I'm no longer a Middle. But at the same time I'm sure I'm not yet a Senior. Every company has different grades. Some rate me as a Middle developer, others as a Senior. It's all just labels. Call me whatever you like — what matters is that I can bring value to the team and, just as importantly, grow productively.
What should your team be like?
Flexible. People shouldn't be afraid to make mistakes. Everyone (well, most of the team) should be open to new things. The team should be able to question its own views.
How soon could you start?
As early as tomorrow, if we both feel we can work great together. If I have doubts about that, I'll keep looking. I have a wonderful wife who's ready to let me sit on her neck and not work at all, so I can look for a job calmly and without rushing. Who knows — maybe by then my wife will already be a senior and I won't have to work at all. 🕺
Where do you see yourself in n years?
A samurai has no goal, only a path. 😉
What value can you bring to a project?
Honestly, any. I've always been interested in how a given product's monetization is built. When we talk, I'll definitely want to learn how your product makes money. Has it paid off yet, or is it still in the startup stage? Either way, I'll understand and sense how I need to act to help your project become more profitable. If I need to sit down and write some backend, I'll roll up my sleeves and write a simple CRUD in Node.js. If you tell me it has to be in Go, I'll sit down and learn Go. Then I'll write the backend. The only thing to keep in mind is that it might be more cost-effective for the business to hire a ready-made backend developer than to ask me to figure out some basic things. That said, I'd get a lot of joy if you gave me the chance to try something completely new. Right now, for example, I'm looking at a couple of openings with an ElectronJS stack. And in the evenings I've started writing a React Native app myself.
I pick things up fast and adapt. Sanguine is my middle name.
